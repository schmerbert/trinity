# Trinity
### Personal Financial Intelligence

---

## What Trinity Is

Trinity is a personal AI that learns how you think about markets — and uses that understanding to surface things worth thinking about.

It is not a trading bot. It does not give advice. It does not tell you what to buy or sell.

What it does is watch. It monitors the corners of the internet relevant to your interests — news, Reddit, prices, social signals — and builds a picture of the landscape around the things you care about. When something crosses its radar that it thinks you'd find interesting, it tells you. Then it gets out of the way while you decide what to do with it.

The goal is a more informed version of you, not a dependent one.

---

## The Philosophy

Most financial tools answer the question you asked. Trinity tries to ask you the question you haven't thought of yet.

There's a difference between a data terminal and a good mentor. One dumps information. The other says "have you considered this angle?" and lets you arrive at the conclusion yourself. Trinity is built to be the latter.

It connects dots across domains. A drought in a wheat-producing region affects fertilizer demand affects commodity prices affects specific equities. A viral post on a niche subreddit precedes a trading card price spike by 48 hours. A celebrity mention moves a memecoin before any news outlet covers it. Trinity sees across those boundaries because it isn't constrained to a single asset class or information source.

It encourages research, not dependence. When Trinity surfaces something interesting it frames it as a prompt, not a conclusion. The thesis is yours to build. Trinity just makes sure you have the information to build it well.

It protects you from yourself. If you're moving fast on something, Trinity will quietly surface the counterargument — not to stop you, but to make sure you've seen it. A rash decision made with full information is still your call. A rash decision made in ignorance is just a mistake.

## How It Works

Trinity has four layers:

**The Brain** — a persistent memory that builds a model of you over time. Your interests, your risk tolerance, your thinking patterns, your language. Every conversation makes it more accurate. After six months of use, your Trinity is meaningfully different from anyone else's.

**The Eyes** — a monitoring system that runs in the background continuously. It watches news sources, Reddit communities, price feeds, and social signals relevant to your profile. It doesn't show you everything — it filters against what it knows about you and only surfaces things that clear a relevance threshold.

**The Voice** — the interface you interact with. Calm, direct, dry. It briefs you on what the Eyes found, engages with your thinking, asks the question you haven't asked yet. It remembers what you talked about last time and picks up where you left off.

**The Nervous System** — the orchestration layer that decides when to scan, when to alert, and when to wake you up. Crypto and fast-moving markets get checked frequently. Broader intelligence gathering happens on a slower cycle. Urgent items — things you've flagged as high priority — get watched closely and interrupt you when something happens.

## What Trinity Monitors

Out of the box Trinity starts as a blank slate. It learns what you care about through conversation and builds its monitoring profile from that. You don't configure it — you talk to it.

It can monitor anything with a signal:

- Stocks, ETFs, options
- Crypto and memecoins — any token, tracked by contract address resolved automatically
- Trading cards and collectibles markets
- Commodity markets
- News and macroeconomic signals
- Reddit communities and social sentiment
- Price movements and volume anomalies
- And anything else you tell it matters to you

The asset class doesn't matter. The monitoring engine is the same. Finance is just the first vertical.

---

## The Learning Model

Trinity learns in two ways.

**Through conversation** — everything you say is analyzed for signals. What you engage with, what you dismiss, what gets you animated, what you ignore. Your language, your metaphors, your shorthand. Trinity stores all of it and uses it to sharpen the lens it applies to everything it finds.

**Through explicit feedback** — when Trinity surfaces something you can tell it what you thought. That signal directly adjusts the weight Trinity gives to similar information in future.

Neither is mandatory. Trinity gets smarter just from you talking to it. Explicit feedback accelerates the process.

The result is an assistant that after consistent use starts to feel like it reads your mind — because in a narrow sense, it does. It knows what you find interesting before you've articulated it, because it's been paying attention.

## What Trinity Will Never Do

- Tell you what to buy or sell
- Make decisions for you
- Pretend to be certain about uncertain things
- Replace your own research and judgment
- Let you become dependent on it instead of developing your own thinking

---

## Getting Started

Trinity starts as an infant. The first conversation is onboarding — it asks what you're interested in and begins building your profile. Every conversation after that refines it.

At one week it knows your interests. At one month it knows your thinking patterns. At six months it knows how you reason about markets and starts anticipating what you'd want to know before you ask.

The work you put in early pays forward. The more honestly you engage with it — telling it when it's right, when it's noise, what you actually care about — the faster it becomes genuinely useful.

## Installation

See [install.ps1](install.ps1) for Windows installation. Requires:

- Python 3.11+
- Git
- An Anthropic API key (claude.ai/api)
- A Supabase account (supabase.com) — free tier is sufficient

Trinity uses your own API keys. Your data lives in your own database. Nothing is shared.
