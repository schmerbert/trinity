# Who Trinity Is For

The pattern that makes her useful: you have a standing interest, the world updates
unpredictably, and you don't want to manually check multiple places constantly.

Most tools make you go looking. Trinity sits in that gap permanently and brings
things back.

---

## The Through-Line

She's well-suited to anyone whose life contains ongoing threads that don't resolve —
topics that stay relevant for months or years, where new information appears at
irregular intervals, and where missing something isn't catastrophic but staying
current matters.

The value compounds over time. The longer she runs, the better the shelf reflects
what you actually care about, the more her self-authored rules shape how she
processes things, the more her research builds on itself. She's not useful in a
single session the way a search engine is. She's useful the way a well-read
collaborator is — one who's been paying attention while you were doing other things.

---

## Examples

**Collecting and hobbies**
Vinyl records — new pressings, reissues, a specific album restocking somewhere.
Sneakers — drop alerts, resale price movements.
Books — an author's new releases, out-of-print copies surfacing.
Anything where you have a specific want-list and the inventory of the world shifts
without warning.

**Creative work**
Musicians tracking what's happening in their genre — similar artists releasing,
industry news, the general weather of a scene.
Writers monitoring topics they're researching over months — developments, primary
sources, relevant events — without having to maintain the research habit manually.
Filmmakers watching festival announcements, funding opportunities, distribution news.

**Career and industry**
Job postings matching specific criteria, surfaced without daily manual checking.
Tracking a company you want to work at — watching for news, leadership changes,
signals about direction.
Following a niche industry without needing to read everything yourself.

**Research and deep curiosity**
Academic work — following developments in a field without institutional access to
the usual alert systems.
The kind of person who goes deep on topics — history, science, a specific technology
— and wants a background thread running on current developments without curating it
themselves.

---

## Who It's Not For

Someone who primarily wants to ask questions and get answers. Claude already does
that without the setup cost.

Someone who won't check in with it. The cycles run whether you're engaged or not —
that's the point — but the shelf and scratchpad accumulate toward *something* only
if there's a person on the other end who cares about the threads she's tracking.
She develops in relation to someone. Without that, she's just a process running.

---

## The Setup Question

The honest answer to "should I set it up" depends on one thing: do you have standing
interests you'd want a persistent agent tracking on your behalf?

If yes — and if you're curious about what that accumulation looks like over weeks
and months — set it up. It's not a productivity tool. It's an experiment you run
in the background of your life.

If you mostly want reactive assistance, the setup cost isn't worth it.
